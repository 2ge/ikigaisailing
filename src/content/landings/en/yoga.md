## What is a yoga retreat on a catamaran like?

You live aboard **Ikigai**, our **Catana 47 catamaran**, anchored in the flat, turquoise lagoons of **San Blas (Guna Yala), Panama**. Each morning opens with yoga and breathwork on the wide trampoline and deck — a catamaran's twin hulls make a far steadier platform than a monohull, so balance work actually feels grounded. Practice is followed by swimming, freediving or simply drifting in 28 °C water. There is no traffic, no wifi pulling at you, and no fixed itinerary — only the rhythm of the tide and the next island.

## What's included in the San Blas yoga retreat?

The experience runs per cabin aboard a Catana 47 (no full-boat group needed): a single berth in a shared cabin, daily morning yoga and breathwork, all meals cooked aboard with fresh fish and local produce, snorkelling and freediving gear, and slow sailing between the San Blas islands with our certified crew. Your skippers are RYA Yachtmaster qualified and AIDA / Apnea Total certified freedivers, so the days blend safe water practice with genuine seamanship. The price is a **member contribution to Ikigai Sailing ASD** — a CONI-recognised, MSP Italia–affiliated non-profit — not a commercial charter fee, which is why a week here costs a fraction of an agency cruise.

## Who is it for?

Anyone who wants to swap the noise of land for a slower, saltier rhythm: first-time sailors, seasoned yogis, solo travellers and couples. The group stays small, so the practice stays personal.
