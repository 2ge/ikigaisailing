## What a San Blas catamaran charter actually is

San Blas — **Guna Yala** on the maps — is an autonomous indigenous territory of roughly 365 islands off Panama's Caribbean coast. The Guna run it themselves: no concrete building, no resorts, a cap on what gets taken from the water. Most of the islands are uninhabited specks of palm and white sand. You reach them properly one way — by boat. We sail it on **Ikigai**, a **Catana 47** catamaran, and we charter it **by the cabin** so you don't need to assemble a group or rent the whole hull.

## Per cabin, not per boat — and why that matters

Whole-boat San Blas charters run €18,000+ a week through agencies, which means you need five or six friends ready to commit and a broker taking a 15–30% cut off the top. Per cabin breaks that. You book one berth. The week includes a bed aboard a Catana 47, all meals cooked onboard from fresh fish and what the islands grow, snorkel and freediving gear, and slow sailing between anchorages with a crew holding RYA Yachtmaster and AIDA / Apnea Total certifications. What you pay is a **member contribution to Ikigai Sailing ASD**, a CONI-recognised, MSP Italia–affiliated non-profit — not a commercial fee. That structure is the whole reason a week here lands at a fraction of the agency rate.

## What you do out here

Freediving in water you can see the bottom of. Yoga on a deck that sits flat. Janzu in lagoons still enough for it. Or nothing — a hammock, a book, the next empty island. The days bend around the weather and what the group wants, not a fixed itinerary.

## A place that still belongs to itself

You won't find this in Bali or Croatia. The lagoons stay warm and flat year-round, most days you won't see another charter, and the culture around you is the Guna's own — molas drying on a line, dugout canoes, a rhythm that predates the marina by a few centuries.
