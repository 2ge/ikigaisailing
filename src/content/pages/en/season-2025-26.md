Why San Blas

**The Guna Yala Archipelago** , also known as **San Blas** , is made up of more than **350 islands and islets** scattered across a turquoise sea.  
These lands, inhabited and protected by the **Guna people** , are one of the last places on Earth where **nature and ancestral culture coexist in harmony**.

We have chosen to settle here from **December 2025 to June 2026** , pausing our continuous sailing for a while, because:

  * **Here, time slows down** : days follow the rhythm of the sun, the tides, and the wind.
  * **Beauty is essential** : no resorts, no tourist marinas. Just palm trees, sand, stars, and silence.
  * **Respect is fundamental** : the Guna live by community rules, deeply connected to the land, the sea, and the spirits. A vision that fully reflects our own commitment to a simple and conscious life.
  * **There’s space for community** : in this remote paradise, we can take the time to build authentic relationships, welcome new members, and share the spiritual and experiential wisdom of life aboard.

**Food for the soul and body** : Thanks to the abundance of fresh fish, tropical fruits, and local vegetables, here we can nourish ourselves in a healthy and natural way, following the rhythms of the environment. It’s a return to simplicity and a deep respect for what nature provides.

WHAT WILL WE DO?

We will live immersed in the pristine nature of the Guna Yala archipelago, where the **sea, wind, and sun** become precious allies in a deep process of rebalancing.  
We’ll sleep rocked by the waves, wake up with the sunlight, and follow a **natural rhythm** with measurable effects on the nervous system, mood, sleep, and vitality.

Each day will be a **journey inside and out** , a return to what truly matters.

We will practice **meditation, yoga, and conscious breathing at sunrise** , training body and mind in harmony, with sessions of **freediving, coral reef swimming** , and **deck or beach workouts**.

We’ll explore deserted islands by **kayak** and **stand-up paddle** , sail through remote anchorages, and visit **Guna communities** to hear their stories, exchange experiences, and discover a culture still in symbiosis with nature.  
We’ll encounter **dolphins, nurse sharks** , and other wonders inhabiting these crystal-clear waters, allowing ourselves to be transformed by the raw, vibrant beauty of this ecosystem.

We will experience the **regenerative power of water** through freediving and practices inspired by **aquatic therapy**.  
You will learn to breathe better, quiet the mind, and **listen to what happens inside** each time your body surrenders to the water.

We’ll nourish ourselves with **fresh, unprocessed food** , prepared with care and love.  
We’ll give space to **authentic needs** , letting go of those that are induced.  
You’ll have time to **slow down, listen to yourself** , and reconnect with your primary sense of being.

Onboard, we’ll cultivate **relationships based on kindness, respect, and deep listening**.  
Together, we’ll spark **virtuous circles of presence, authenticity, and sharing**.

And when it’s time to go home, you’ll take something more with you:  
the **memory of a simple, intense life** , and perhaps a **clearer compass** to navigate even on land.

Together, we’ll try to **return to the essential** , nourishing ourselves with what is true, alive, and shared.  
It’s not just an escape.  
**It’s a return to yourself.**
