## Just slip in

Snorkelling in San Blas needs nothing more than a mask, a pair of fins and the will to put your face in the water. No tank, no certification, no course. The lagoons are warm — around **28 °C** — shallow, and clear enough to read the sand from the deck. You step off the back of the catamaran and the reef is right there.

## Reefs no day-tour reaches

Because we sail by the cabin and anchor where we like, you snorkel spots the day-boats never bother with — coral heads, drop-offs, sandbanks where rays glide past. Most days you'll share them with nobody. Float over a reef left alone for centuries, watch a nurse shark drift below, then climb back aboard for lunch. It's the easiest door there is into the underwater world, and in San Blas it opens onto one of the richest.
