## Learn to sail by sailing

Most of what people call a "sailing course" happens at a desk. Here it happens at sea. *Ikigai* is a **Catana 47** — a real ocean-going catamaran — and every passage between islands is a lesson if you want it to be. You take the helm, trim the sails, learn to read the wind on the water and the clouds on the horizon, and stand watch on longer hops. Our skipper holds an **RYA Yachtmaster** certification, so what you learn is the real thing, taught the way it's actually done.

## From first tack to night watch

Never touched a winch? You'll start with the basics — points of sail, how a catamaran behaves, how to hold a course. Already sail? You'll go deeper: trim for speed, weather routing, anchoring in a crowded lagoon, and the quiet competence of a night watch under stars with no light for miles. There's no syllabus to rush — you learn at the pace the sea sets.
